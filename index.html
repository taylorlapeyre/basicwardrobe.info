A (Frugal) Basic Wardrobe

The goal of this website is to present a minimal capsule wardrobe for men with frugal pieces. Most, if not all, of the items will be under $100. Check the original site for higher quality items.


Bottoms | Tops | Outerwear | Shoes | Formal Attire | Extras

  • Bottoms

  • One Pair of Jeans

    • A good pair of dark denim is a great choice for almost all occasions. Raw denim is nice to have but it does come with a higher price. Washed undistressed denim is a good, cheap alternative.

    • Suggestions:
    • Levi's 501 Shrink-to-Fit (Mid Range)
    • Uniqlo Skinny Fit Tapered Jeans (T000) (Mid Range)
    • Unbranded Indigo UB201 (High End)

  • Two Pairs of Chinos

    • Cheap chinos are easy to find, but cheap chinos that fit is another story. Avoid buying cheap but loose chinos and focus on buying something that fits.

    • Suggestions:
    • Dockers Alpha Khaki (Mid Range)
    • Uniqlo Vintage Chino Flat Front Pants (Mid Range)

  • Three Pairs of Shorts

    • When it gets hot, you want shorts. Looks great with a casual button up but make sure the fit of your shorts matches with the fit of your shirt.

    • Suggestions:
    • Stafford Prep Chino Shorts (Low End)
    • Uniqlo Belted Shorts (Mid Range)

  • Tops

  • Three Oxford Cloth Button Downs

    • Pretty much every company has their own version of the oxford cloth button down. Look for good fit and how fine the weave is. A tighter weave works for more formal occasions while looser weaves should be left to the weekend.

    • Suggestions:
    • jcp Oxford Shirt (Low End)
    • Uniqlo Oxford Long Sleeve Shirt (Mid Range)

  • Two Patterned Button Ups

    • Casual button up shirts are great for the weekend and sometimes work well with a more formal outfit. Again, fit is key, especially if you are wearing your shirts untucked.

    • Suggestions:
    • Land's End Canvas Heritage Mini Gingham Poplin Shirt (Mid Range)
    • J. Crew Factory Washed Button Down Shirt in Tattersall (Mid Range)

  • Four T-Shirts

    • Finding a basic, unadorned, well fitting, cheap t-shirt is, for some reason, hard to find. Avoid graphic tees and look for something slim.

    • Suggestions:
    • Mossimo Supply Co. Men's Short Sleeve Tee (Low End)
    • Alternate Apparel Boss V Neck (Mid Range)
  • Two Sweaters

    • Sweaters are ubiquitous in winter time but also can be worn as a layering piece in the spring or fall. Look for something thin for the warmer days and something chunky when it gets colder. Cashmere is pretty expensive so stick with wool or wool blends.

    • Suggestions:
    • jcp Fine Gauge Cardigan (Low end)
    • Uniqlo Lambswool Sweater (Low End)

  • Outerwear

  • A Light Jacket

    • Good method of layering in the fall and spring seasons. Keeps the wind out and keeps you a bit warmer at night.

    • Suggestions:
    • Uniqlo UIP Block Tech Jacket (Mid Range)
    • LL Bean Signature Flight Jacket (High End)
  • A Coat

    • Don't buy cheap oversized coats. Buy a classic piece that lasts a long time.

    • Suggestions:
    • Uniqlo Wool Blended Pea Coat (Low End)
    • J. Crew University Coat (Mid Range)

  • Shoes

  • Boots

    • Boots are one of the places where it does not pay to cheap out. Look for high quality construction and full grain leather. Leather soles for a more sleek, professional look and rubber soles for a more workwear look.

    • Suggestions:
    • Clark's Beeswax Desert Boot (Low End)
    • Thorogood Plain Toe Boots (Mid Range)
    • LL Bean Katahdin Iron Works Engineer Boots (High End)
    • Aldo Mr.B Blake Wingtip Boots (High End)
  • Sneakers

    • Try to avoid logos in sneakers and look for a sleek design. Construction matters a bit less here.

    • Suggestions:
    • Vans Canvas Authentic (Low End)
    • Supra Cutter Low "Two" (Mid Range)
    • Converse Jack Purcells (High End)
  • Casual Leather Shoes

    • Suede bucks, boat shoes and wingtips are the foundataions of your casual shoe collection. My opinion is that higher quality and construction is not as important here as with more formal or more rugged shoes.

    • Suggestions:
    • Stafford Ashton Wingtip Shoes (Low End)
    • Bass Suede Bucks (Low End)
    • Sperry Boat Lite 2-Eye (Mid Range)

  • Formal Attire

  • One Navy Blazer

    • There are a lot of cheap blazers in the market. Look for one that is well fitting and consider changing the buttons if you aren't satisfied with the current look.

    • Suggestions:
    • Uniqlo Wool Blended Jacket (Mid Range)
  • Two Dress Shirts

    • Avoid shiny materials and stick with classic poplin or broadcloth. Make sure that it fits well and the thickness of the shirt is to your liking.

    • Suggestions:
    • Brooks Brothers Extra Slim Fit Striped Dress Shirt (High End)
    • Lands End Tailored Fit (Low End)
  • A Suit

    • If you don't wear suits very often, you can get a little cheap here, but if you do, look into something higher end. When looking at suits, don't just look at the softness of the fabric, look at the construction and quality of the materials.

    • Suggestions:
    • J. Crew Factory Charcoal Thompson Suit (Low End)
    • Suit Supply Havana 3P Plain (Mid Range)
  • Ties

    • Again, if you don't wear ties everyday, you can cheap out a bit. Look for something that feels solid and makes a good knot. Lower quality ties will get more wrinkled easily.

    • Suggestions:
    • The Tie Bar (Low End)
    • Land's End (Mid Range)
  • Dress Shoes

    • This is where you cannot afford to be cheap. Cheap shoes just look cheap, use inferior leather and are poorly constructed. Try to get second quality shoes from established shoe brands such as Allen Edmonds or wait for sales.

    • Suggestions:
    • Allen Edmonds Park Avenue (High end)
    • Meermin Dark Brown Calf Oxford (High end)

  • Extras

  • A Rain Coat

    • Trench coats and technical shells are a great addition to your wardrobe. If you're in the rain often, look into re-waterproofing your jacket once in a while.

    • Suggestions:
    • Patagonia Torrentshell (Low End)
    • Uniqlo Trench Coat (Mid Range)
  • Rain Boots

    • Rainboots are something to wear to work to keep your good leather shoes from getting damaged. Utility over fashion for this one.

    • Suggestions:
    • L.L. Bean - Bean Boots (Low End)
  • Watches

    • A watch adds some visual flair to your wrist and shows off a bit of your personality. Make sure the size of the watch fits your wrist.

    • Suggestions:
    • Timex Slip Thru Weekender (Low End)
    • Seiko 5 SNK807 (Mid Range)
